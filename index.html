How to Find the Best Plumber in Chicago: Step by Step

The process of finding the right plumber does not have to be overwhelming. Follow these steps in order and you will quickly narrow the field to only qualified, trustworthy candidates.

1
Verify Their City of Chicago Plumbing License

Illinois state licensing alone is not enough. Chicago requires its own city-issued plumbing license. Check the City of Chicago Business Affairs & Consumer Protection portal to confirm the license is active before any conversation goes further.

2
Confirm They Carry Liability Insurance and Workers Comp

If a worker is injured on your property or a repair causes water damage to a neighbor's unit, you could be held liable. Always ask for a certificate of insurance before work begins — reputable companies send it without hesitation.

3
Read Chicago-Specific Reviews on Google and Yelp

Filter reviews for mentions of response time, honest pricing, and whether the plumber cleaned up after the job. Reviews from Chicago neighborhoods carry more weight than generic star ratings because local code knowledge matters here.

4
Request a Written, Itemized Estimate

A verbal quote means nothing legally. Any trustworthy plumber provides a written estimate that breaks down labor, parts, and permit fees separately. If they refuse or pressure you to decide on the spot, walk away.

5
Compare at Least Two or Three Quotes

For any job over $300, get multiple estimates. Prices in Chicago can vary significantly between contractors for identical work. The lowest quote is not always the best — weigh experience, reviews, and warranty terms alongside the price.

6
Ask About Warranty on Parts and Labor

A skilled, confident plumber backs their work. Ask specifically: "Do you offer a warranty on this repair, and for how long?" Standard warranties range from 30 days to one year. Get the answer in writing as part of your estimate.

Questions to Ask Before You Hire

  • ✓Are you licensed with the City of Chicago, not just the state of Illinois?
  • ✓Can you send a certificate of liability insurance before the job starts?
  • ✓Will you pull the required permits if this job needs them?
  • ✓Do you provide a written, itemized estimate before any work begins?
  • ✓What warranty do you offer on parts and labor?
  • ✓Have you worked on homes of this age and pipe type before?
  • ✓What is your estimated arrival window for this type of job?

⚠ Red Flag: Any plumber who demands full cash payment upfront, refuses to show a license, or gives a quote over the phone without seeing the job should be avoided. These are classic signs of an unqualified operator.

How to Handle a Plumbing Emergency While You Wait

  • 1Turn off the main water shutoff — in most Chicago homes it is in the basement near the front foundation wall
  • 2Shut off your water heater to prevent dry-fire damage once the supply is cut
  • 3Open a cold faucet on the lowest floor to drain residual pressure from the lines
  • 4Take clear photos and video of visible damage for your insurance claim
  • 5Call a licensed plumber — not a general handyman — for any issue involving supply lines, drain stacks, or sewer connections

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I verify a plumber's Chicago city license?

Visit the City of Chicago Business Affairs & Consumer Protection website and use the license lookup tool. Enter the plumber's name or company name and confirm their license status is active before you hire.

Do I need a permit for plumbing work in Chicago?

For anything beyond basic fixture replacement — such as moving drain lines, replacing a water heater, or touching the main supply or sewer stack — a permit is required. A licensed plumber handles this as part of the job scope.

How quickly can a plumber arrive in Chicago for an emergency?

Most established Chicago companies respond within 60 to 90 minutes for emergencies. Always confirm the ETA when you call, and ask whether the technician dispatched holds a city license — not just the company.

Is a solo plumber as reliable as a large company?

Yes — provided they carry the proper city license, liability insurance, and workers compensation. Many solo operators offer excellent craftsmanship at lower overhead costs. Verify credentials the same way regardless of company size.
